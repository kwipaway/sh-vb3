Using Military, Veteran, and Dependent Education Benefits at SHSU

This website is designed to help students at Sam Houston State University understand what education benefits they may use, who qualifies, and what steps to take to start using those benefits successfully.

Explore Benefits

Who This Guide Is For

This guide is for students at SHSU who are:

Veterans

Former service members using earned education benefits after military service.

Active Duty Military

Currently serving students who may use Tuition Assistance or VA education benefits.

Dependents

Spouses and children who may qualify for transferred benefits or dependent education programs.

Education Benefits Students May Use at SHSU

Post-9/11 GI Bill (Chapter 33)

Helps pay tuition and fees and may also provide a monthly housing allowance and book stipend.

Montgomery GI Bill (Chapter 30)

Provides education payments directly to eligible students and can be used toward approved programs.

Veteran Readiness & Employment (Chapter 31)

Supports eligible veterans with service-connected disabilities through education and career preparation.

Dependents' Educational Assistance (Chapter 35)

Available to certain spouses and dependents of veterans who are disabled or deceased due to service.

Selected Reserve GI Bill (Chapter 1606)

Supports members of the Selected Reserve who qualify for education assistance while serving.

Hazlewood Exemption

A Texas education benefit that may exempt qualified veterans and eligible dependents from certain tuition and fees.

Military Tuition Assistance

Active duty service members may use Tuition Assistance through their military branch to help pay for classes.

How to Start Using Benefits at SHSU

1

Apply for Your Benefit

Apply through the VA, Texas Hazlewood program, or your military branch.

2

Get Approval Documents

Collect documents such as your Certificate of Eligibility or Hazlewood paperwork.

3

Submit Documents to SHSU

Send your documents to SHSU’s Veterans Benefits Processing office.

4

Register for Classes

Enroll in courses that apply to your degree plan.

5

Monitor Your Status

Check your student account and stay in contact with SHSU offices.

Common Student Questions

Will all benefits pay the same way?

No. Some benefits pay tuition directly to the school, while others may pay the student directly.

Does Hazlewood pay for housing or books?

No. Hazlewood generally applies to eligible tuition and fee exemptions only.

Can dependents use benefits at SHSU?

Yes, if they qualify through transferred GI Bill benefits, Chapter 35, or Hazlewood Legacy.

Do active duty students use the same process as veterans?

Not always. Active duty students may need approval from their military branch first.
